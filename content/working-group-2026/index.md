## Licensing as Infrastructure for Open Science

Research is shifting toward a future where knowledge is no longer communicated solely through traditional papers and preprints. Instead, research is increasingly produced as a collection of modular outputs, including datasets, figures, code, notebooks, methods, and other research components that can be shared and reused independently.

While many of these outputs are technically shareable, reuse often remains difficult in practice. Researchers regularly encounter uncertainty around licensing, attribution, provenance, and reuse permissions. As a result, valuable research components may remain underused, poorly cited, or disconnected from the broader research record.

Licensing has the potential to help address these challenges. When licensing information is clear, visible, and interoperable with research infrastructure, it enables researchers to confidently reuse research outputs while maintaining attribution and provenance. Licensing can therefore function not only as a legal mechanism but also as an enabling layer of infrastructure that supports responsible reuse across the research ecosystem.

The Reuse Incentives Working Group explores how licensing systems and complementary infrastructure can reduce friction and enable reuse across modular research outputs. The group examines both technical and cultural dimensions of reuse, including licensing practices, attribution systems, identifiers, metadata, and platform design.

## Working Group Goals

The Reuse Incentives Working Group is focused on three primary goals.

### Identify reuse pain points

The group seeks to document the challenges researchers encounter when attempting to reuse modular research outputs and to identify examples where licensing has successfully reduced friction.

### Explore licensing as an enabler of reuse

The working group examines how licensing systems, together with complementary infrastructure such as persistent identifiers, metadata, and attribution frameworks, can support confident reuse of modular research components.

### Develop recommendations for modular research reuse

The group will produce a framework and set of recommendations to support responsible reuse across modular research outputs including datasets, code, figures, notebooks, and other research components.

### Meeting Context

The working group is exploring how research might move beyond traditional paper-based publishing toward **modular research objects**.

These modular research outputs may include:

- Datasets
- Figures and tables
- Notebooks and code
- Methods and protocols
- Design files or lab notes

In this model, research outputs exist as interconnected components rather than as a single static paper. Individual research objects can be shared, reused, cited, and recombined independently.

Within this evolving research environment, licensing and provenance information become essential infrastructure. Clear licensing signals help researchers and machines understand how research objects can be reused, while provenance information supports attribution, trust, and reproducibility across platforms and tools.

The working group is examining how licensing can function not only as a legal framework but also as an operational layer of research infrastructure that enables reuse across disciplines, tools, and platforms.

## Working Group Participants

The working group brings together participants from across the research ecosystem, including researchers, technologists, librarians, publishers, infrastructure builders, and open science advocates.

These participants contribute perspectives from across scholarly communication, open infrastructure, and research practice. Their combined experience helps the working group examine how licensing and reuse operate across different disciplines, research workflows, and technological systems.
